Team History

The team was founded in 1917 and were called the Toronto Arenas. In 1919, it was renamed to the Toronto St. Patricks. At last, the team was purchased by Conn Smythe which was then renamed to the Toronto Maple Leafs. The team’s jersey’s have also been altered throughout the changes. The team has won 13 Stanley Cup championships throughout the team’s career. The team won their last Stanley Cup championship in 1967 having the longest drought between championships in the NHL. The Toronto Maple Leafs are currently affiliated with the Toronto Marlies of the AHL (American Hockey League). The team’s first home arena was the Arena Gardens in 1917 and Maple Leaf Gardens in 1931. Their home arena then went to the Air Canada Center now known as the Scotiabank Arena. Current Players

The 2022 current Toronto Maple Leafs’ roster comprises of 24 players including the Team Captain, John Tavares, the Alternate Captains, Auston Matthews, Mitch Marner, and Morgan Rielly, and the goalies, Jack Campbell and Petr Mrazek. There are five centermen, five right wingers, four left wingers, eight total defensemen, and two goalies on the team. The team has not had a team captain since 2016 until John Tavares was traded from the New York Islanders in 2018 and became captain at the start of the 2019 season. Auston Matthews and Mitch Marner were announced alternate captain at the same time of Tavares. Morgan Rielly has been the longest alternate captain for the team since 2016.
